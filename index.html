About Our Department

The Department of Mechanical Engineering at P.T. Lee Chengalvaraya Naicker Polytechnic College is dedicated to delivering quality education and hands-on training, empowering students to become skilled engineers ready for industry demands.

Our Laboratories

  • Computer Integrated Manufacturing Lab (CIM)
  • Solid Modeling Lab
  • Production Drawing & Modeling Lab (PDM)
  • Advanced Manufacturing Technology Practical Lab

Vision & Mission

Institutional Vision

To become a model polytechnic institution by offering both curriculum-based and job-oriented courses that equip students with the skills needed to excel in engineering and adapt to technological advancements.

Institutional Mission

  • Promote academic excellence, self-discipline, and high ideals for holistic personality development.
  • Transform novices into competent global technologists through dedicated teamwork.

Department Vision

  • Provide quality education to Mechanical Engineering students, enhancing employability and technical expertise.

Department Mission

  • Educate students on core Mechanical Engineering concepts via effective teaching-learning methodologies.
  • Continuously improve academic performance and technical skills through hands-on practical sessions.
  • Enhance employability, entrepreneurship, and leadership qualities via industry exposure.

Computer Aided Design Laboratory – Do's & Don'ts

DO's

  • Follow all lab rules and schedules.
  • Handle equipment with care.
  • Save your work frequently.
  • Use only licensed, approved software.
  • Organize and name files properly.
  • Maintain a quiet, disciplined environment.
  • Log off and tidy up your workstation after use.
  • Request assistance if you encounter issues.
  • Respect others' work and intellectual property.
  • Report any equipment or software problems immediately.

DON'Ts

  • Don't install or uninstall any software.
  • Don't eat or drink near computers.
  • Don't use lab computers for personal browsing.
  • Don't tamper with hardware or system settings.
  • Don't share your login credentials.
  • Don't use unapproved USB drives or devices.
  • Don't plagiarize or copy others' designs.
  • Don't save files in unsecured or random locations.
  • Don't distract or disturb other students.
  • Don't ignore lab safety guidelines or emergency protocols.

Computer Integrated Manufacturing Lab

PART A: SOLID MODELLING

  1. Geneva Wheel
  2. Bearing Block
  3. Bushed Bearing
  4. Gib and Cotter Joint
  5. Screw Jack
  6. Universal Coupling

PART B: CNC PROGRAMMING & MACHINING

  1. Linear & Circular Interpolation – Create part program and produce component.
  2. Stock Removal Cycle – Program multiple turning operations and produce component.
  3. Canned Cycle – Program for thread cutting, grooving; produce components.
  4. Combined Interpolation – Program grooving using linear & circular interpolation; produce component.
  5. Canned Cycle – Program drilling, tapping, counter-sinking; produce components.
  6. Subprogram Usage – Create part program with subprograms; produce component.

Advanced Manufacturing Technology Practical

PART A: SOLID MODELLING & CNC PRACTICALS

  1. Linear & Circular Interpolation – Create part program; produce component.
  2. Stock Removal Cycle – Program multiple turning operations; produce component.
  3. Canned Cycle – Thread cutting & grooving; produce component.
  4. Combined Interpolation – Grooving using linear & circular; produce component.
  5. Canned Cycle – Drilling, tapping, counter-sinking; produce component.
  6. Subprogram – Create part program with subprogram; produce component.
  7. 3D Printing / Simulation – Model Geneva Wheel and export STL.
  8. 3D Printing / Simulation – Model C-Clamp with V-Block and export STL.
  9. Robot Arm – Record point-to-point motion (5 positions) in a simulator.
  10. Robot Arm – Record continuous motion (straight, circular, inclined) in a simulator.
